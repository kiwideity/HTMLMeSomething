Lessons from Christchurch



Like most New Zealanders I was stunned by the attack on the Christchurch mosques that killed 50 people including a 3 and 4 year old child. I weep at the senseless violence and loss of precious lives.
As-Salaam-Alaikum.
I contrast this to my carefree childhood and mourn the loss of my former countries innocence.
The policewoman in this picture is wearing a head scarf in solidarity with the moslem community and a rose to mourn those lost in the  tragedy. Many New Zealand women have followed the prime ministers lead and are wearing headscarves to show their support for the New Zealands moslem community and New Zealanders have formed human chains of protection around mosques for Friday prayers.


When asked by President Trump what the US could do to help, the prime minister Jacinda Ardern responded "by showing love and compassion to moslems everywhere". Its bittersweet that such a tragedy has led to my my proudest moments as a Kiwi. "They are us"

My US Life

I came to the US in early 2006 with my two year old son in tow.

 

We soon found acceptance and love within the St Louis community and I married an American late that year. Unfortunately my husband became chronically ill two years into our marriage and got progressively worse over the next 7 years and died on 5/23/17.

Miss you Baby

I was overwhelmed by the love and support of my colleagues and the Washington University community.
Since then my son and I have healed and are embracing life in the US. I very much enjoy my work at the university and the camaraderie of my colleagues who as smart as they are amusing. My son loves the US and is in every way an American. I'm still a bit of a hybrid but am learning to love my adopted country and to enjoy its many splenders. We have a good life here and are grateful for the many opportunities this country offers. We continue our journey toward becoming US citizens and look forward to contributing to this fine country.

Thank you USA.

My Kiwi Life

I was born in New Zealand and grew up in a beautiful country known for its friendliness and hospitality. Each year the first of the spring lambs are displayed proudly on the front page of the Waikato Times.

Long summers were spent at the beach running wild, swimming, fishing and generally experiencing a freedom my son will never know.

 

As I got older I enjoyed horse riding through many of New Zealands Forests.

Hiking through many of its nature trails.
